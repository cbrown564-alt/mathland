The baseline

Flat gray admin aesthetic. No brand identity. Three callout colors, no system. The character — the whole differentiator — is a 22px gray circle. Reads as a content-management dashboard, not a learning world.

03

Path A — Stabilize & polish

Same structure, same screens — but consistent, correct, and considered. A real token layer, one accent system, working buttons, loading skeletons, unified chrome. This is the "stop the bleeding" path: low risk, makes everything that exists feel intentional. It will not make the product beautiful or distinctive — but it makes every subsequent path possible.

04

Path B — Let the characters lead

The differentiating move. The assets already exist — 11 animated GIFs (60MB, currently orphaned), voiced audio, per-character color systems, distinct personalities with their own verbs. Path B's job is to surface them: make characters persistent companions, re-theme the whole interface per character, and turn the home screen into a world where each character guards a territory of math.

Path B

Character as companion

Vera is present — her purple/pink tints the whole screen, she speaks in her own voice (her verbs, her catchphrase), her animated portrait sits in the companion rail with a live "speaking" indicator while audio plays, and her interactive is embedded inline rather than exiled to a tab. The 60MB of orphaned GIFs finally have a home here.

05

Path C — Re-architect the lesson

The structural bet. Today a lesson is a sidebar of 8 discrete sections that each render one isolated card — it reads like a form. Path C rethinks the lesson as a continuous, guided narrative: the character walks you through Hook → Read → See → Hear → Do as one flowing story, audio persistently underneath, the interactive arriving as the climax where everything converges. Think Brilliant meets Headspace — linear, paced, never overwhelming.

06

The three, side by side

What each path delivers, what it costs, and who it's for.

Stabilize & polish

2–4 weeks · low risk
  • Fixes the JIT purge, dead buttons, placeholder content
  • One token layer, one component catalog, unified chrome
  • Makes what exists feel intentional and correct
  • Doesn't change the fundamental look or feel
Table stakes. Do this regardless.

Characters lead

1–2 months · medium risk
  • Surfaces 60MB of orphaned animation + voices
  • World-map home, per-character re-theming
  • Converts wasted assets into visible differentiation
  • The one thing competitors can't easily copy
The visible overhaul. Highest leverage.

Re-architect

2–3 months · high risk
  • Lesson as guided narrative, not section form
  • Interactive as climax, adaptive pacing
  • Transforms "course website" into "experience"
  • Biggest bet on rethinking pedagogy itself
The long game. Prototype one lesson first.
Recommendation

Do A now, commit to B as the overhaul, stage C as a prototype.

Path A is correctness, not style — the JIT bug and dead buttons undermine trust every time a user hits them, so it happens regardless of direction. Path B is where ambition pays off: it converts your biggest wasted asset (characters, voices, animation) into your biggest visible asset, and nobody else can match it. Path C is worth it only if the product finds traction — de-risk it by redesigning one lesson end-to-end first.
