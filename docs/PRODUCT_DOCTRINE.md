# Mathland product doctrine

Status: **canonical product direction**, accepted 12 July 2026.

Mathland is an open mathematical world for motivated adults rebuilding mathematics so they can reach more advanced subjects. It is not a school course, a remedial programme, a content catalogue, or a lightly improved version of the current application shell.

## The promise

Mathland helps adults acquire mathematical power for a harder thing they already care about.

Learners should be able to enter through a meaningful case in physics, engineering, machine learning, AI, or finance; discover the mathematical structure inside it; take respectful detours through missing foundations; and return able to recognise the same structure in a different context.

## Primary learner

The primary learner:

- is an adult relearning mathematics rather than encountering it for the first time;
- has a concrete ambition such as understanding control systems, machine learning, quantitative finance, or advanced physics;
- brings substantial real-world knowledge and can reason from authentic cases;
- is highly motivated and willing to work hard when the work feels purposeful;
- prefers active learning, experimentation, case studies, and consequential analogies;
- expects adult language, intellectual honesty, and visible depth;
- may have uneven prerequisite knowledge and should not be forced through an entire school syllabus before reaching the motivating problem.

## Product architecture

The accepted architecture is **Atlas outside, Studio inside, Observatory selectively**.

- **Atlas:** the open world. It shows mathematical territories, prerequisite relationships, domain routes, unfinished questions, and useful next moves.
- **Studio:** the default learning surface. Learners predict, manipulate, calculate, explain, revise, transfer, and retrieve.
- **Observatory:** a selective opening for ideas that benefit from wonder, scale, motion, or a strong visual reveal. It creates attention, then hands the learner to the Studio.

These are product responsibilities, not visual themes. The detailed contract is in [EXPERIENCE_ARCHITECTURE.md](EXPERIENCE_ARCHITECTURE.md).

## Product laws

1. **Action before exposition.** Begin with a question, prediction, case, or manipulable model. Explain into attention that the learner has already earned.
2. **One cognitive job per moment.** Keep the relevant words, representations, and controls together. Remove anything that competes with the current mathematical move.
3. **Agency with a visible horizon.** Learners can choose goals, cases, and routes while always knowing where they are and what their choices imply.
4. **Practice changes shape.** Move from model and worked example to faded support, independent solution, and cross-context transfer.
5. **Memory is a return loop.** A viewed lesson is not a mastered idea. Important structures return through spaced and mixed retrieval.
6. **Detours are diagnostic, not remedial.** Missing prerequisites appear when learner action reveals a need, then route back to the motivating goal.
7. **Formalism follows purpose without losing rigour.** Intuition and cases create a place for notation to attach; they do not replace proof, precision, or calculation.
8. **Accessibility is interaction design.** Every essential interaction works without drag alone, focus remains obvious, and navigation and help are predictable.
9. **Characters are field specialists.** They own tools, language, questions, and ways of seeing. They do not decorate content or infantilise the learner.
10. **Transfer is the outcome.** Success means recognising and using a mathematical structure beyond the example that introduced it.
11. **The unfamiliar product teaches itself.** A skippable, reopenable first-run tour demonstrates the complete working loop before expecting independent navigation.
12. **Wrong answers begin instruction.** Preserve the attempt, locate the first meaningful divergence, and escalate support without converting supported work into independent evidence.
13. **Domain changes are announced.** Transfer and retrieval separate domain orientation from the mathematical attempt, and allow deferment or equivalent-context substitution when vocabulary blocks access.
14. **Interaction and exposition share the load.** Mathematical objects lead, while precise definitions, derivations, theorems, worked reasoning, and sustained explanation remain visible when they are the strongest medium.
15. **Media must change what the learner can notice.** A guide, illustration, audio clip, or video earns its place only by teaching a mathematical move and providing a complete accessible alternative.

## Journey and evidence contract

- A learner chooses one active horizon per journey. It persists through Studios, Atlas visits, detours, returns, and reloads; changing it is a deliberate action that never discards work.
- Routine task orientation stays compact enough to keep the mathematical object visible in the first viewport. Display-scale spectacle is reserved for rare Observatory openings.
- Support escalates through observation, focused cue, comparison, worked step, diagnostic detour, and a fresh equivalent attempt.
- Evidence names both the action and the support used: independent, observed, cued, compared, worked, restudied, deferred, or substituted. Page views and interface locks never create capability claims.
- Previewing a future retrieval prompt is preparation, not delayed-retrieval evidence.

## Entry paths into one world

Domain paths are motivating entrances, not separate course catalogues.

### Physics and engineering

Start with motion, forces, structures, fields, trajectories, robot arms, and control systems. Natural routes include vectors, matrices, calculus, differential equations, and optimisation.

### Machine learning and AI

Start with embeddings, similarity, recommendations, classifiers, uncertainty, and model training. Natural routes include vectors, linear algebra, probability, statistics, calculus, and optimisation.

### Finance

Start with cashflows, portfolio return, risk, inference, and decisions under uncertainty. Natural routes include functions, linear algebra, probability, statistics, stochastic models, and optimisation.

The Atlas must reveal that these paths repeatedly cross the same mathematical territories.

## What is explicitly superseded

The following are not foundations for the rebuild:

- the current homepage, dashboard, shared shell, navigation, and route hierarchy;
- the eight-section lesson flow;
- Lesson v2 as a presumed destination;
- module cards or a ten-module linear sequence as the learner's primary mental model;
- completion percentages as a proxy for understanding;
- characters used mainly as mascots, colour themes, or scripted encouragement;
- generic gamification, decorative spectacle, and school-grade framing;
- mass migration of the existing 96 lessons before the new experience is validated.

Existing content, characters, and interactives are raw material. Each element must earn its way into the new world against this doctrine.

## Evidence of success

Product success should be evaluated through learner behaviour and transfer:

- learners can orient themselves and choose a meaningful next move;
- learners act frequently and receive mathematically useful feedback;
- learners can explain an idea in words, notation, and a relevant representation;
- learners can apply a structure in a different domain from the one that introduced it;
- prerequisite detours repair gaps without breaking motivation or creating shame;
- learners can resume after time away and retrieve important ideas;
- keyboard, touch, screen-reader, reduced-motion, and narrow-screen journeys remain complete;
- characters and cases make the mathematics more intelligible, not merely more memorable.

## Decision rule

When a proposed feature conflicts with this document, change or reject the feature. Do not weaken the doctrine to preserve legacy implementation.
